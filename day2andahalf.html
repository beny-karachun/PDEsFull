Day 2.5: Existence and Uniqueness (First-Order PDEs) - משפט הקיום והיחידות

Theory Focus

Existence and Uniqueness Theorem (via Characteristics):

For the first-order quasi-linear PDE $a(x,y,u)u_x + b(x,y,u)u_y = c(x,y,u)$, with initial data $u=u_0(s)$ prescribed on a curve $\Gamma$ parametrized by $s$ as $(x_0(s), y_0(s))$, a unique solution exists in a neighborhood of the curve $\Gamma$ provided certain conditions hold.

Key Condition (Transversality Condition):

The crucial condition derived from the Method of Characteristics is that the initial curve $\Gamma$ must **not** be tangent to the characteristic curves projecting onto the $(x,y)$ plane at any point.

Mathematically, let the initial curve projection be $\vec{\Gamma}(s) = (x_0(s), y_0(s))$. The tangent vector to the curve is $\vec{\Gamma}'(s) = (\frac{dx_0}{ds}, \frac{dy_0}{ds})$. The projection of the characteristic direction at a point on the curve is $\vec{V}(s) = (a(x_0, y_0, u_0), b(x_0, y_0, u_0))$.

The condition for existence and uniqueness is that these two vectors are not parallel. This is typically checked using a determinant (often called the Jacobian of the transformation from $(s,t)$ characteristic coordinates to $(x,y)$ coordinates, evaluated at $t=0$):

$$ J = \det \begin{pmatrix} \frac{\partial x}{\partial t} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial t} \\ \frac{\partial x}{\partial s} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial s} \end{pmatrix} \Bigg|_{t=0} = \det \begin{pmatrix} a(x_0, y_0, u_0) & b(x_0, y_0, u_0) \\ \frac{dx_0}{ds} & \frac{dy_0}{ds} \end{pmatrix} \neq 0 $$

This simplifies to the **transversality condition**: $$ a(x_0, y_0, u_0) \frac{dy_0}{ds} - b(x_0, y_0, u_0) \frac{dx_0}{ds} \neq 0 $$

Why? If the initial curve is characteristic ($J=0$), the characteristic curves originating from it either overlap (potentially leading to infinitely many solutions) or carry contradictory information (leading to no solution). When the condition holds ($J \neq 0$), the characteristics cross the initial curve transversally, ensuring that initial data propagates uniquely into a neighborhood.

Assumptions: This holds locally, provided the coefficients $a, b, c$ and the initial data parametrization $(x_0(s), y_0(s), u_0(s))$ are continuously differentiable ($C^1$). This guarantees the existence of characteristic curves and well-defined tangents.

Practice Questions

Question 1:

PDE: $u_x + u_y = 0$. Initial Curve $\Gamma: x_0(s)=0, y_0(s)=s$. Does a unique local solution exist near $\Gamma$?

Solution:

$a=1, b=1$. Curve: $x_0=0, y_0=s$. Tangent: $\frac{dx_0}{ds}=0, \frac{dy_0}{ds}=1$.

Check condition: $a \frac{dy_0}{ds} - b \frac{dx_0}{ds} = (1)(1) - (1)(0) = 1$.

Since $1 \neq 0$, a unique local solution exists.

Question 2:

PDE: $u_x + u_y = u$. Initial Curve $\Gamma: x_0(s)=s, y_0(s)=s$. Initial data $u_0(s)=\cos(s)$. Does a unique local solution exist near $\Gamma$?

Solution:

$a=1, b=1$. Curve: $x_0=s, y_0=s$. Tangent: $\frac{dx_0}{ds}=1, \frac{dy_0}{ds}=1$.

Check condition: $a \frac{dy_0}{ds} - b \frac{dx_0}{ds} = (1)(1) - (1)(1) = 0$.

Since the condition equals 0, the initial curve is characteristic. A unique local solution is **not** guaranteed. (As seen in Day 2 Q9, no solution exists unless $u_0$ was constant).

Question 3:

PDE: $xu_x + yu_y = 0$. Initial Curve $\Gamma: x_0(s)=1, y_0(s)=s$. Does a unique local solution exist near $\Gamma$?

Solution:

$a=x, b=y$. Curve: $x_0=1, y_0=s$. Tangent: $\frac{dx_0}{ds}=0, \frac{dy_0}{ds}=1$.

Evaluate $a, b$ on the curve: $a(x_0,y_0,u_0)=x_0=1$, $b(x_0,y_0,u_0)=y_0=s$.

Check condition: $a \frac{dy_0}{ds} - b \frac{dx_0}{ds} = (1)(1) - (s)(0) = 1$.

Since $1 \neq 0$, a unique local solution exists.

Question 4:

PDE: $yu_x - xu_y = 0$. Initial Curve $\Gamma: x_0(s)=s, y_0(s)=0$. Does a unique local solution exist near $\Gamma$ for $s \neq 0$?

Solution:

$a=y, b=-x$. Curve: $x_0=s, y_0=0$. Tangent: $\frac{dx_0}{ds}=1, \frac{dy_0}{ds}=0$.

Evaluate $a, b$ on the curve: $a(x_0,y_0,u_0)=y_0=0$, $b(x_0,y_0,u_0)=-x_0=-s$.

Check condition: $a \frac{dy_0}{ds} - b \frac{dx_0}{ds} = (0)(0) - (-s)(1) = s$.

Since $s \neq 0$ is given, the condition $s \neq 0$ holds. A unique local solution exists near $\Gamma$ (away from $s=0$).

Question 5:

PDE: $u_x + u_y = 1$. Initial Curve $\Gamma: x_0(s)=s, y_0(s)=-s$. Does a unique local solution exist near $\Gamma$?

Solution:

$a=1, b=1$. Curve: $x_0=s, y_0=-s$. Tangent: $\frac{dx_0}{ds}=1, \frac{dy_0}{ds}=-1$.

Check condition: $a \frac{dy_0}{ds} - b \frac{dx_0}{ds} = (1)(-1) - (1)(1) = -1 - 1 = -2$.

Since $-2 \neq 0$, a unique local solution exists.

Question 6:

PDE: $u_t + c u_x = 0$. Initial Curve $\Gamma: t_0(s)=0, x_0(s)=s$. Does a unique local solution exist near $\Gamma$?

Solution:

Here independent variables are $x, t$. PDE is $cu_x + 1u_t = 0$. $a=c, b=1$.

Curve: $x_0=s, t_0=0$. Tangent: $\frac{dx_0}{ds}=1, \frac{dt_0}{ds}=0$.

Check condition (using $t$ for $y$): $a \frac{dt_0}{ds} - b \frac{dx_0}{ds} = (c)(0) - (1)(1) = -1$.

Since $-1 \neq 0$, a unique local solution exists.

Question 7:

PDE: $u_t + x u_x = 0$. Initial Curve $\Gamma: t_0(s)=s, x_0(s)=e^s$. Does a unique local solution exist near $\Gamma$?

Solution:

PDE is $xu_x + 1u_t = 0$. $a=x, b=1$.

Curve: $x_0=e^s, t_0=s$. Tangent: $\frac{dx_0}{ds}=e^s, \frac{dt_0}{ds}=1$.

Evaluate $a, b$ on the curve: $a=x_0=e^s, b=1$.

Check condition: $a \frac{dt_0}{ds} - b \frac{dx_0}{ds} = (e^s)(1) - (1)(e^s) = e^s - e^s = 0$.

Since the condition equals 0, the initial curve is characteristic. A unique local solution is **not** guaranteed.

Question 8:

PDE: $u_x + uu_y = 0$. Initial Curve $\Gamma: x_0(s)=0, y_0(s)=s$. Initial data $u_0(s)=s$. Does a unique local solution exist near $\Gamma$?

Solution:

$a=1, b=u$. Curve: $x_0=0, y_0=s$. Tangent: $\frac{dx_0}{ds}=0, \frac{dy_0}{ds}=1$.

Evaluate $a, b$ on the curve: $a=1$, $b=u_0(s)=s$.

Check condition: $a \frac{dy_0}{ds} - b \frac{dx_0}{ds} = (1)(1) - (s)(0) = 1$.

Since $1 \neq 0$, a unique local solution exists.

Question 9:

PDE: $u_x + yu_y = u$. Initial Curve $\Gamma: x_0(s)=s^2, y_0(s)=1$. Does a unique local solution exist near $\Gamma$?

Solution:

$a=1, b=y$. Curve: $x_0=s^2, y_0=1$. Tangent: $\frac{dx_0}{ds}=2s, \frac{dy_0}{ds}=0$.

Evaluate $a, b$ on the curve: $a=1, b=y_0=1$.

Check condition: $a \frac{dy_0}{ds} - b \frac{dx_0}{ds} = (1)(0) - (1)(2s) = -2s$.

A unique local solution exists provided $s \neq 0$. At $s=0$ (which corresponds to the point $(0,1)$), the condition fails.

Question 10:

PDE: $u_x - y u_y = 0$. Initial Curve $\Gamma: x_0(s)=\ln s, y_0(s)=1/s$ for $s>0$. Does a unique local solution exist near $\Gamma$?

Solution:

$a=1, b=-y$. Curve: $x_0=\ln s, y_0=1/s$. Tangent: $\frac{dx_0}{ds}=1/s, \frac{dy_0}{ds}=-1/s^2$.

Evaluate $a, b$ on the curve: $a=1, b=-y_0=-1/s$.

Check condition: $a \frac{dy_0}{ds} - b \frac{dx_0}{ds} = (1)(-1/s^2) - (-1/s)(1/s) = -1/s^2 + 1/s^2 = 0$.

Since the condition equals 0, the initial curve is characteristic. A unique local solution is **not** guaranteed.

(Note: $ye^x = (1/s)e^{\ln s} = (1/s)s = 1$. The characteristics are $ye^x=C$. The initial curve lies entirely on the characteristic $ye^x=1$.)

Question 11:

PDE: $u_x + x u_y = u$. Initial curve $\Gamma: x_0=0, y_0=s$. Check condition.

Solution:

$a=1, b=x$. Curve $x_0=0, y_0=s$. Tangent $dx_0/ds=0, dy_0/ds=1$.

On curve: $a=1, b=x_0=0$.

Check: $a(dy_0/ds) - b(dx_0/ds) = (1)(1) - (0)(0) = 1 \neq 0$. Unique local solution exists.

Question 12:

PDE: $u_x - u_y = 0$. Initial curve $\Gamma: x_0=s, y_0=s^2$. Check condition.

Solution:

$a=1, b=-1$. Curve $x_0=s, y_0=s^2$. Tangent $dx_0/ds=1, dy_0/ds=2s$.

On curve: $a=1, b=-1$.

Check: $a(dy_0/ds) - b(dx_0/ds) = (1)(2s) - (-1)(1) = 2s + 1$.

Unique local solution exists provided $2s+1 \neq 0$, i.e., $s \neq -1/2$. Condition fails at $s=-1/2$.

Question 13:

PDE: $u_x + u_y = 0$. Initial curve $\Gamma: x_0=\cos s, y_0=\sin s$. Check condition.

Solution:

$a=1, b=1$. Curve $x_0=\cos s, y_0=\sin s$. Tangent $dx_0/ds=-\sin s, dy_0/ds=\cos s$.

On curve: $a=1, b=1$.

Check: $a(dy_0/ds) - b(dx_0/ds) = (1)(\cos s) - (1)(-\sin s) = \cos s + \sin s$.

Unique local solution exists provided $\cos s + \sin s \neq 0$, i.e., $\tan s \neq -1$. Condition fails when $s = -\pi/4 + k\pi$.

Question 14:

PDE: $xu_x - yu_y = 0$. Initial curve $\Gamma: x_0=s, y_0=1/s$. Check condition for $s\neq 0$.

Solution:

$a=x, b=-y$. Curve $x_0=s, y_0=1/s$. Tangent $dx_0/ds=1, dy_0/ds=-1/s^2$.

On curve: $a=x_0=s, b=-y_0=-1/s$.

Check: $a(dy_0/ds) - b(dx_0/ds) = (s)(-1/s^2) - (-1/s)(1) = -1/s + 1/s = 0$.

Condition fails for all $s \neq 0$. The initial curve is characteristic. Unique local solution not guaranteed.

(Note: Characteristics are $xy=C$. The initial curve $y=1/x$ is the characteristic $xy=1$.)
